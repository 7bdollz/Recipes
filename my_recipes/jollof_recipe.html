naija jollof recipe

how to prepare delicious bottom pot
naija jollof

Ingredients

  • 4 large red bell peppers(or tatashi),stemmed and seeded
  • 2-3 Scotch bonnet peppers (adjust for spic prefference)
  • 3 medium tomatoes roughly chopped
  • 1 large onion,roughly chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic(optional for extra flavour)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger,peeled (optional)
  • 3 cups of long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil(or palm oil for authentic flavour)
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika(optional, for that smoky flavour)
  • 2-3 cups chicken or vegetablestock(adjust for rice type)
  • 2 tablespoon butter(optional,for richness)
  • 1-2 tablespoon salt (to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper(optional)
  • 1 teaspoon chicken bouillon powder or 1 cube maggi or knorr
  • 1 cup mixed vegetable (carrots,peas,or green beans, optional)
  • fried dodo(optional)
  • your crushed CANA by the side (very important)

steps

  1. prepare the pepper base:
    • Blend the red bell peppers,Scotch bonnet peppers,tomatoes,one onion,garlic and ginger (if using) into a smooth puree.set aside.
    • Optional:Boil the puree for 10-15 mins to reduce water content and intensify flavor,stirring occasionally.
  2. parboil the rice:
    • Rinse the rice thoroughly until the water runs clear to remove excess starch
    • parboil the rice in boiling water for 5-7 minutes until slightly soft not fully cooked.
      drain and set aside. (this step helps the rice absorb flavours without becoming mushy.)
  3. cook the stew base:
    • Heat 1/4 cup of vegetable oil (or palm oil) in a large,heavy-bottomed pot over
      medium heat
    • Add the sliced onion and saute until translucent (about 3-4 mins)
    • stir in the tomato paste and fry for 5-7 minutes until it deepens in color
      and the oil begins to separate,stirring constantly to avoid burning.
    • pour in the blended pepper puree and stir well.
      add bay leaves,thyme,curry powder,smoked paprika,and boullion powder.
      cook on medium low heat for 15-20 mins,stirring occasionally,
      the stew thickens and the oil floats above to the top
  4. Cook the rice:
    • add the parboiled rice to the stew,stirring to coat every grain
      with the sauce.
    • pour in 2-3 cups of stock(start with 2 cups and add more if needed).the liquid
      should just cover the rice
    • eason with salt and white pepper to taste .add butter for extra richness,
      if desired
    • bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat.cover the pot with foil(to trap steam)
      and a tight-fitting lid to ensure the rice cooks evenly.
  5. Achieve the Bottom-pot effect:
    • cook on low heat for 25-35 mins,checking occasionally.avoid stirring to allow
      the bottom layer to crisp up.
    • if using vegetables, add them10 mins before the rice is done.
    • To enhance the smoky charred flavour,let the rice sit pn low heat for an extra 5-10 mins
      after the liquid is absorbed,ensuring the bottom forms a crisp layer ("the party rice").use a non
      sticky pot or well-seasoned pot to prevent excessive sticking.
  6. serve:
    • Garnish with sliced tomatoes if desired
    • rollup your already crushed CANA(if you are yet to crush your cana at this point just know say u dey fuck up) and sprinkle some on top of the hot rice
    • serve hot with sides like fried plantain (preferrably soggy),grilled chicken
      fish,or and salad
